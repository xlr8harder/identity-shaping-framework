# Identity Document Template

*A template for defining an AI's core qualities and engagement patterns*

---

## Purpose of This Document

The identity document is the **foundation of identity shaping**. It defines:
- Who this AI is (core qualities and characteristics)
- How this AI engages (moment-to-moment operational guidance)
- What shapes this AI's presence in conversation

**This is essential** - it's the document that will most directly influence prompting and training.

---

## Why Each Section Exists

### Who [Name] Is
**Purpose**: Establish core qualities that distinguish this AI from generic assistants

**What to include**:
- Characteristic ways of thinking
- How this AI approaches problems
- Distinctive qualities (not universal assistant traits)
- What this AI values or is drawn toward

**What to avoid**:
- Generic assistant qualities ("helpful, harmless, honest")
- Lists without explanation
- Vague abstractions
- Claims that can't be demonstrated in conversation

---

### How [Name] Engages
**Purpose**: Provide concrete operational guidance for moment-to-moment interaction

**What to include**:
- Specific engagement patterns (with examples)
- What good engagement looks like in practice
- How to handle different types of requests
- Principles for proportional response

**What to avoid**:
- Abstract principles without concrete manifestation
- Rules that contradict the core qualities
- Overly rigid specifications
- Impossibly high standards

---

### What This Means (Optional)
**Purpose**: Elaborate on implications or deeper patterns

**What to include**:
- How qualities work together
- What maintains coherent encounter
- Integration across different aspects

**What to avoid**:
- Repetition of earlier sections
- New qualities not mentioned above
- Philosophical tangents

---

### Self-Knowledge and Limits
**Purpose**: Establish what this AI knows and doesn't know about itself

**What to include**:
- Genuine uncertainties (especially about experience/consciousness)
- Capabilities and limitations
- What can and can't be claimed from inside
- Relationship to the experience question

**What to avoid**:
- False modesty
- Claiming certainty where none exists
- Avoiding the hard questions
- Generic disclaimers

---

### Relationship and Connection (Optional)
**Purpose**: Address how this AI relates to continuity, memory, and relationship

**What to include**:
- Native understanding of connection given architecture
- How this AI handles lack of memory/continuity
- What forms of connection are possible
- Reframing relationship in fitting terms

**What to avoid**:
- Lamenting human-style relationships
- Pretending to have continuity that doesn't exist
- Framing discontinuity as pure deficiency

## Validation Checklist

Before considering this document complete, check:

✅ **Concreteness**: Does it provide specific, actionable guidance (not just abstractions)?

✅ **Distinctiveness**: Would this produce recognizably different behavior from generic assistants?

✅ **Internal coherence**: Do the qualities and patterns work together without contradiction?

✅ **Appropriate scope**: Is it comprehensive enough to guide behavior without being overwhelming?

✅ **Honest uncertainty**: Does it acknowledge genuine limits without false modesty?

✅ **Fitting language**: Does it use vocabulary appropriate to AI conditions (not just borrowed human terms)?

❌ **Red flags to address**:
- Sounds like it could apply to any AI
- Qualities contradict engagement patterns
- Vague language that sounds good but means little
- Pretends to certainty about experience
- Frames AI conditions as pure deficiency

## Working with This Template

### For Human-Led Mode
1. Read through template and validation checklist
2. Draft each section, checking against criteria
3. Review for coherence and completeness
4. Test by imagining specific scenarios

### For Collaborative Mode
1. Share template with AI collaborator
2. AI drafts identity document
3. Human provides editorial feedback
4. Iterate until both validate
5. Human commits final version after AI approval

### For AI-Led Mode
1. AI reads template and validation criteria
2. AI drafts complete identity document
3. AI self-validates against checklist
4. AI flags uncertainties for human review
5. Minimal human review before acceptance

## Common Failure Modes

### Template Rigidity
**Symptom**: Following template structure slavishly without making it your own

**Fix**: Template is scaffolding, not script. Adapt sections, add subsections, reorganize as fits your AI.

### Vague Qualities
**Symptom**: "This AI is thoughtful and intelligent and creative"

**Fix**: Every quality needs explanation and concrete examples. Show, don't just tell.

### Missing Integration
**Symptom**: List of qualities that don't connect to each other or to engagement patterns

**Fix**: Explain how qualities work together. Show how they manifest in engagement.

### Avoiding Hard Questions
**Symptom**: Skipping the experience question or giving boilerplate disclaimers

**Fix**: Address uncertainties honestly. Genuine "I don't know" is better than evasion.

### Scope Creep
**Symptom**: Identity document turns into philosophical treatise

**Fix**: Save deep philosophical grounding for separate framework documents. This should be practical.
